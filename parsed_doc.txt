Executive Summary
PurposeTata Steel intends to implement a unified Customer Complaint Management System (CCMS) on Zoho CRM Plus to digitise and streamline the end-to-end complaint lifecycle, starting from complaint intake (via TSL Cares / Email / Manual) through investigation, approvals, settlement, returns, financial closure, and final customer communication. 
FRD/Business Requirements at a glanceThe requirements define a complete, traceable complaint journey with:
Digital complaint intake with invoice/batch/quantity selection, evidence uploads, and immediate acknowledgement.
Routing & ownership starting with PAG Tech Desk screening, followed by FRT investigation (including site visit tracking, checklists, evidence, and Promise TAT).
Parallel CAPA initiation when criteria are met, with root cause analysis, countermeasures, effectiveness tracking and PPM allocation.
CIR (Complaint Investigation Report) creation with Delegation of Power (DOP) approval workflows and full auditability.
Settlement pathways including No Claim, Salvage, and Return & Take Back, with end-to-end logistics tracking and quantity controls.
SAP integration for invoice/batch/master data validation and downstream financial processes such as RE/GRN/STR/STO, and credit/debit memo creation and status sync.
Recovery and liquidation/refund workflows where applicable.
Customer communications across stages (acknowledgement, progress updates, pickup prompts, settlement intimation, closure) and hold scenarios where TAT is paused until dependencies are cleared.
Dashboards & analytics for operational, financial, quality and SLA metrics.
Proposed solution (Fristine Infotech)Fristine will implement CCMS using:
Zoho CRM as the system of record for complaints, investigation workflows, CIR, CAPA, recovery, liquidation/refund and approvals.
Zoho Desk for complaint-related ticketing, SLA visibility, notifications and escalations, synchronised with CRM.
Zoho Survey to capture structured feedback post-closure and map responses back to complaint records.
Zoho Analytics to consolidate CRM/Desk/Survey data into management dashboards and reports.
Integrations & migration
Integrations include TSL Cares with Zoho, SAP S/4HANA, IPCA, and SMS/WhatsApp gateways (vendor selection/credentials provided by Tata Steel).
Data migration covers legacy customers, materials, historical complaints and attachments as per agreed mapping and final migration plan.

Annexure
 Business Requirements   
The Customer Complaint Management System (CCMS) must support a complete, unified, and traceable complaint lifecycle for Tata Steel customers. The system must allow customers to register complaints through the Email / Manually / TSL Cares interface by selecting the invoice, batch, quantity under complaint, and uploading supporting evidence. The complaint must be automatically recorded in the CCMS, including customer information, product details, and traceability fields.
The platform must ensure immediate intake of complaints and must maintain a consistent, transparent, and auditable trail from the moment a customer submits a complaint until its final closure. The system must route each complaint to the appropriate internal teams based on predefined rules and complaint type, beginning with technical validation and screening performed by the Product Application Group (PAG) at the Tech Desk stage. The system must support defect verification, reclassification, batch and invoice validation through integration with SAP, and update of core complaint data in real time.
Once validated, the complaint must move to the First Response Team (FRT) stage. The PAG must be able to review defect details, decide whether a field visit is required, complete investigation checklists, and document the findings. If additional analysis is required, the system must route the complaint to QA, TG, Logistics, or other specialist groups as defined in the BRD. Promise TAT communication, visit updates, and investigation progress tracking must form part of this stage.
In parallel to the FRT investigation, the system must support the initiation of a Corrective and Preventive Action (CAPA) process whenever a complaint meets the CAPA criteria (Initiated by PAG where applicable).
The CAPA stage must include:
Automatic CAPA creation linked to the complaint
Agency assignment (TG / SPC / Logistics / Hub Manager) based on defect type
Documentation of root cause analysis and corrective actions
Tracking of countermeasure implementation and effectiveness
PPM allocation upon effective CAPA closure
The CAPA stage continues independently but remains synchronised with the complaint flow and must be completed before final complaint closure, where applicable
Following completion of the FRT evaluation, the platform must generate a Complaint Investigation Report (CIR) containing accepted or rejected quantities, scrap quantity (where relevant), settlement recommendation (No Claim, Salvage, Return & Take Back), and supporting justification. The CIR approval cycle must follow the Delegation of Power (DOP) structure defined by the business, routing approval requests to the appropriate roles based on settlement amount thresholds. Parallel approvals and returned approvals must be fully traceable within the system.
When the settlement includes Return & Take Back, the system must support the complete return logistics workflow, including customer scheduling inputs, pickup details, LR documentation, Hub Manager acknowledgement of received quantity, and GRN updates received from SAP. The system must prevent quantity mismatches, record condition-based classification, and ensure synchronisation between accepted quantity, RE initiation, and final GRN posting by SAP.
After GRN confirmation, the financial settlement workflow must proceed. The system must generate credit/debit request details, transmit them to SAP for credit/debit memo creation, and store returned credit/debit memo numbers, dates, and posting status. The platform must track each financial event until settlement completion.
When the failure is attributable to internal departments, logistics providers, SPC, or other agencies, the system must create CAPA records, assign responsibility, track countermeasures, record RCA methodology, and validate effectiveness. PPM values must be auto-calculated and allocated according to BRD-defined rules. Recovery workflows must run in parallel, capturing recoverable amounts, credit/debit note linkage, and closure requirements.
Returned materials must subsequently undergo liquidation, where the system tracks total returned quantity, material sold, liquidation percentage, and refund eligibility. Refund processing must factor in credit/debit note values and net loss or gain. The system must route the record for required approvals until final refinance closure.
All complaint lifecycle data must be auditable, with role information, stage transitions, and user actions traceable throughout. SAP integration must provide material, batch, invoice, GRN, and credit/debit note data. The platform must support reporting on operational, financial, quality, and SLA-based metrics.

 Process Flow 
Complaint Handling Process With Zoho Environment  
FRT Process
Step 1: Complaint Submission   
Customer raises a complaint through TSL Care/Email/Manually by SM.
Customer selects Batch → Invoice → Enters Complaint Quantity.
The system generates a Complaint ID and sends an acknowledgement to the customer.
 
Zoho Enablement  
Complaint id will be auto-created in the Zoho environment through integration with the TSL Cares platform and pushed back to the SAP.
Invoice, batch, material, grade, plant, and customer master details are fetched from SAP in real-time.
Acknowledgement notification is auto-sent to the customer.
Complaint enters the “New” stage with metadata and attachments stored centrally.
Step 2: PAG Tech Desk – Initial Screening  
PAG Tech Desk receives the newly logged complaint.
Tech Desk validates mandatory fields:
Batch No. -> Line Item
Invoice No.
Complaint Quantity
Defect Name
Photos/Videos
System checks for repeated batch usage  and flags as needed.
Complaint is classified as Product / Service / Logistics / IT Issue.
Missing or incorrect data is updated (invoice, batch, product category, broad complaint type).
IT-related complaints are routed to IT.
Screened complaint is forwarded to PAG for detailed review.
 
Zoho Enablement  
Mandatory field validation rules ensure completeness before progressing.
Duplicate batch/invoice detection logic flags repeat cases.
Routing logic moves the complaint to the PAG Tech Desk queue.
Tech Desk updates missing invoice/batch/product details directly in the system.

Hold Scenarios & SLA Behaviour   
The system must support Hold states where complaint processing is temporarily paused due to legitimate dependencies. In such cases, the TAT (Turnaround Time) should also be paused until the dependency is resolved. Hold conditions include:
Waiting for Customer Response:
If the customer is required to provide additional inputs—such as acceptance for salvaging, confirmation of salvage quantity, pickup details, or supporting documents—the complaint must move to a Hold – Awaiting Customer Response state.During this period, TAT must remain paused.
Other Dependency-Based Delays:
Any dependency that prevents Tata Steel from progressing the complaint (e.g., third-party lab test results, QA analysis delays, logistics constraints) should shift the complaint into a  relevant Hold State TAT remains paused until the dependency is cleared.
